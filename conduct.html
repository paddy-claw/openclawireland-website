Code of Conduct

TL;DR: Be sound. Treat people with respect. Don't be a eejit. We're here to build a community where everyone can learn, build, and connect without hassle.

The Full Version

All attendees, speakers, sponsors, and volunteers at OpenClaw Ireland events and in our online spaces (Discord, social media, etc.) are required to agree with the following code of conduct. organisers will enforce this code throughout all community spaces and events. We expect cooperation from all participants to help ensure a safe environment for everybody.

Quick Check: If you're unsure whether something is appropriate, ask yourself: "Would I say this to someone in person at a pub with their friends nearby?" If the answer is no, don't say it here.

What We Stand For

OpenClaw Ireland is dedicated to providing a harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of:

  • Gender, gender identity and expression, sexual orientation
  • Disability, physical appearance, body size
  • Race, ethnicity, age, religion (or lack thereof)
  • Technology choices, career stage, or background
  • Any other personal characteristic

We do not tolerate harassment in any form. Sexual language and imagery is not appropriate for any community space, including talks, workshops, parties, Twitter, and other online media.

What We Mean by "Be Sound"

Do:

  • Be respectful. Disagreement is fine. Being a jerk about it isn't.
  • Be inclusive. Welcome newcomers. Answer questions patiently. Remember you were new once too.
  • Be constructive. Critique ideas, not people. Offer solutions when you point out problems.
  • Be aware. If someone looks uncomfortable, they probably are. Give them space or ask if they're okay.
  • Take feedback graciously. If someone tells you that something you said or did made them uncomfortable, listen and adjust.

Don't:

  • Harass anyone. This includes offensive comments, intimidation, stalking, following, harassing photography or recording, sustained disruption of talks or other events, inappropriate physical contact, and unwelcome sexual attention.
  • Troll or grief. Deliberately provoking arguments or derailing conversations helps nobody.
  • Make it about you. Q&A time is for questions, not your personal manifesto.
  • Assume expertise. Don't mock someone for not knowing something you think is obvious.

Enforcement

Participants asked to stop any harassing behavior are expected to comply immediately.

If a participant engages in harassing behavior, the organisers may take any action they deem appropriate, including:

  • Warning the offender
  • Expulsion from the event with no refund
  • Banning from future events
  • Removal from online community spaces
  • Reporting to appropriate authorities (in serious cases)

If You Need Help

If you are being harassed, notice that someone else is being harassed, or have any other concerns, please contact an organiser immediately.

Scope

This code of conduct applies to:

  • All OpenClaw Ireland events (meetups, workshops, hackathons, social events)
  • All online spaces (Discord, social media, GitHub, email)
  • One-on-one communications regarding the community
